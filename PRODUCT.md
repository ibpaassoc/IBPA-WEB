# Product

## Register

split

The codebase serves two registers from one project: a public marketing/membership site (`(public)` route group — brand: design IS the product) and an internal admin/dashboard tool (`(admin)`, `(dashboard)` route groups — product: design SERVES the product). Pick the register per task based on which surface is in focus; default to `brand` for `(public)` work and `product` for `(admin)`/`(dashboard)` work.

## Users

- **Public visitors & prospective/current members**: people learning about IBPA (the association/board this site represents), browsing news, events, governance, membership criteria, and standards. They also apply for membership, pay dues, and register for events — moving from "learn and engage" to "apply and transact".
- **Internal staff/admins**: review membership applications, manage member profiles and content (articles, events, registrations), and run mailing/campaign communication flows through the admin dashboard. Their context is task-driven, repetitive daily workflows where clarity and speed matter more than persuasion.

## Product Purpose

IBPA-WEB is the public face and operational backbone of the association: it presents IBPA to the world (mission, governance, standards, news, events) and converts interest into membership and event participation, while giving staff the tools to manage that membership, content, and outreach behind the scenes. Success looks like visitors finding what they need quickly, prospective members completing applications without friction, and staff running member/content/campaign operations without fighting the tools.

## Brand Personality

Professional and trustworthy — credible, polished, institutional in the best sense: an established association that takes its standards seriously. Voice is clear and confident, not promotional. On the admin side, the same trustworthiness translates to predictability and precision in the tools staff rely on daily.

## Anti-references

Should NOT look dated or bureaucratic — avoid the stale, form-heavy, default-template feel common to association/government sites. Professional and trustworthy does not mean boring or behind the times; the site should read as a credible organization that is current and well-run, not stuck in the past.

## Design Principles

- **Credibility through clarity**: every page should make IBPA's authority and standards legible at a glance — through structure and typographic confidence, not decoration.
- **Frictionless conversion**: the path from "learning about IBPA" to "applying for membership" or "registering for an event" should be short, clear, and free of unnecessary steps.
- **Tools that disappear**: admin/dashboard surfaces should get out of staff's way — predictable patterns, fast scanning, minimal cognitive overhead for repetitive daily tasks.
- **Current, not trendy**: feel professionally up-to-date without chasing SaaS or startup visual cliches that would undercut institutional trust.
- **One system, two registers**: maintain a coherent visual identity across public and admin surfaces while letting each register's interaction patterns serve its distinct job (persuasion vs. task completion).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA across both registers: ≥4.5:1 contrast for body text (≥3:1 for large text), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any animation. No additional known user-specific requirements beyond AA defaults at this time.
